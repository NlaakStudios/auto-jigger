Introduction

I have worked too many jobs to remember over the last Twenty-Seven years. I have a vast basic knowledge of almost everything technical with a passion in two areas. Software Engineering and Computer/Electronics Engineering. With my Military career now behind me I am looking to to provide my experience as a Software Project Manager.

I believe a great project manager manages the project’s team, the project’s scope, enforcing or improving upon the project’s budget and handles any issues that arise and can remain your reliable and knowledgeable single point of contact to the senior leadership.

My Though pattern is one of broad strokes. This means that I can see a very big picture and plan well ahead and as events happen I focus on a pre-planned road and the brush strokes be finer as I get either closer to the goal or things have are getting out of hand and need to be reigned in.

I have an extremely broad range on experiences from basic electronics to nano technology; from punch cards to PC's to Phones to Mainframes. With software I have experiences with Machine code, Assembler Language as well as Pascal, COBAL, BASIC, C, Java, JavaScript, HTML5, CSS3 MySQL, BASH, etc. I have experience with Artificial Intelligence, Fuzzy Logic, Probabilities, Neural Networks and Expert Systems. I have experience with game Programming, Network communication, Networking, Routers, Access Points and Fiber Optics.

I am also from the days of 16k Computers, so just because storage is massive and cheap now - that does not stop me from creating the smallest most efficient code that I can achieve. I believe in efficiency and not wasting anything. Give me a well defined project, a budget and a time-frame and I will produce.

Some Common Interview Questions

  • In Your Experience, What One Skill Does a Project Manager Need to Succeed?

    Vision.

  • What Methods Will You Use to Deliver the Results We’re Looking For?

    The Global Workforce.

  • How Will You Put Together Your Project Team?

    I use oDesk. I have individuals around the world that have worked with me for the last decade. I tend to balance my team with senior experienced professionals and young, fresh and creative individuals.

  • What software have you used to manage projects in the past?

    MS Office, Skype, Bugzlla, Filezilla, ProjectLibe

  • If I gave you a laptop to plan your next project, what software would you want on it?

    Windows 7 Professional, MS Office, Zend Server, Skype, Bugzlla, Filezilla, ProjectLibre, Cygwin

  • What’s your approach to managing a project?

    I will accept the project as it is given to me. I will study it and research similar (if any) projects out there. I will learn and possibly come up with some new idea and changes. I will confer with my leadership, usually verbally at first and if all is well submit the official changes in documentation. Once that is completed I will use a meticulous planned out process with checkpoints and a extensive Quality Assurance which includes to stages in-house and closed limited end-user. I do this with firm leadership, yet I have a rapport with the team members. I delegate and move on unless there is a issue with the team member in which case they will have to be replaced.

  • What’s your school of thought on project management -- are you an agile person?

  • How do you do your scheduling?

    I use oDesk for Managing My Team(s)

  • How do you allocate resources?

    I use a combination of ProjectLibre and oDesk.

  • How do you do status updates?

    It depends on my leadership. For casual updates I prefer quick emails, for milestones it will be a complete documentation of the current status at that point.

  • How do you handle politics?

    The military bearing within me will not tolerate it. The team exists for a reason, a task, nothing else. Be professional.

  • Do you manage people and projects or just projects?

    I manage both. I have no issues with people, although sometimes projects can be very annoying!
